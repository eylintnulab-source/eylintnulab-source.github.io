Computational chemistry for materials, reactions, and molecular design

Research Overview

Welcome to the EYL Computational Group in NTNU Chem! Our group focuses on theoretical investigation and computational design on solid state materials as well as small discrete systems. We have a broad interest in applying first-principles studies to investigate the structural, electronic, and luminescence properties of various periodic and molecular systems.

We apply computational chemistry to understand catalytic chemical and electrochemical reaction mechanisms with an aim to help modify, optimize, and design new nano or energy materials, including fuel cells, CO2 recycling, and energy storage systems. We also collaborate with experimental partners to achieve a more profound microscopic understanding.

Overall, we seek to deduce empirical rules or design principles from computational examinations that not only echo but also guide multidisciplinary experimental chemistry.

First-Principles Materials

Structural, electronic, magnetic, and luminescence properties in periodic and molecular systems.

Catalysis and Electrochemistry

Reaction mechanisms for fuel cells, CO2 recycling, oxygen reduction, and related catalytic systems.

Energy and Nano Materials

Computational design of energy storage systems, carbon-based nanomaterials, and molecular spintronic candidates.
